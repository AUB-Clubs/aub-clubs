## College Hall:
* Departments/ majors: Administration, Admissions, Financial Aid, Provost's Office.
* Key features: The iconic clock tower. It is the oldest, most recognizable administrative building on campus. Rebuilt after the 1991 bombing.
* Location in respect to main gate: Directly facing the Main Gate. You can't miss it.

## Assembly Hall:
* Departments/ majors: N/A (Events, Concerts, Ceremonies).
* Key features: Historic church-like architecture, features a massive operational pipe organ. 
* Location in respect to main gate: Immediately to the left upon entering the Main Gate.

## Daniel Bliss Hall:
* Departments/ majors: FAS (Computer Science, Mathematics, Chemistry, Humanities).
* Key features: Classic stone architecture, one of the original campus structures. Houses various tech and engineering labs.
* Location in respect to main gate: Just west of College Hall. 

## Post Hall:
* Departments/ majors: Geology, AUB Archaeological Museum.
* Key features: Houses the oldest archaeological museum in the Middle East. Heavy, classic masonry.
* Location in respect to main gate: To the immediate right of the Main Gate.

## Nicely Hall:
* Departments/ majors: FAS (Humanities, CVSP, English).
* Key features: U-shaped building, heavy foot traffic, staple for freshman and sophomore core requirements.
* Location in respect to main gate: West of the Main Gate, directly facing the Green Oval.

## Fisk Hall:
* Departments/ majors: FAS (Education, English, History, Arabic, CAMES).
* Key features: Traditional red-roofed architecture, overlooks the Green Oval. Heavy reading/writing hub.
* Location in respect to main gate: West of the Main Gate, past Assembly Hall.

## West Hall:
* Departments/ majors: Student Affairs, Student Representative Council (SRC), University Clubs.
* Key features: The hub of student life. Contains a food court, Mahmoud Malhas Common Room, and various auditoriums for extracurriculars.
* Location in respect to main gate: West of the Main Gate, adjacent to the Green Oval.

## Jafet Memorial Library:
* Departments/ majors: N/A (Main Campus Library).
* Key features: The central nervous system for studying. Massive archives, quiet reading rooms, 24/7 study areas during finals, and a very high cat population outside.
* Location in respect to main gate: North (downhill) from the Main Gate, centrally located past the Green Oval.

## Issam Fares Institute (IFI):
* Departments/ majors: Public Policy and International Affairs.
* Key features: Ultra-modern, deconstructivist design by Zaha Hadid. A massive concrete cantilever structure with zero 90-degree angles.
* Location in respect to main gate: Northwest of the Main Gate, wedged between Jafet Library and the Green Oval.

## Bechtel Engineering Building:
* Departments/ majors: MSFEA (Civil, Mechanical, Industrial Engineering).
* Key features: The old-school engineering backbone. Heavy machinery labs, 3D printing facilities, and large concrete courtyards.
* Location in respect to main gate: Down the main stairs from Jafet Library; middle-lower campus.

## Irani Oxy Engineering Complex (IOEC):
* Departments/ majors: MSFEA (Electrical, Computer, Chemical Engineering).
* Key features: Modern, tech-heavy labs. Where the CCE/ECE crowds basically live. Great AC, hardware labs, and study spaces.
* Location in respect to main gate: Lower campus, sea side, adjacent to IFI and Bechtel.

## Raymond Ghosn Hall & Dar Al-Handasah Building:
* Departments/ majors: MSFEA (Architecture and Graphic Design).
* Key features: Massive drafting studios, plotting rooms, architecture exhibition spaces. High sleep-deprivation zone.
* Location in respect to main gate: Lower campus, near Bechtel and the engineering complex.

## Munib and Angela Masri Building:
* Departments/ majors: MSFEA (Multidisciplinary engineering research).
* Key features: Sleek, modern hub for advanced engineering research and interdisciplinary work. 
* Location in respect to main gate: Lower campus, adjacent to the main MSFEA buildings.

## Suliman S. Olayan School of Business (OSB):
* Departments/ majors: Business (Finance, Management, Accounting, Marketing).
* Key features: High-end, corporate-style building. Features the Maamari Auditorium, its own cafeteria, and aggressive networking energy.
* Location in respect to main gate: Far lower campus, directly adjacent to the Mediterranean Sea and the Corniche.

## Charles W. Hostler Student Center (CHSC):
* Departments/ majors: Athletics and University Sports.
* Key features: State-of-the-art sports complex. Gym, indoor swimming pool, basketball courts, track, and green-roof architecture.
* Location in respect to main gate: Lower campus, right on the seafront, west of OSB.

## Van Dyck Hall:
* Departments/ majors: Faculty of Health Sciences (FHS).
* Key features: Classic AUB stone architecture, features the Nabil Bustani Auditorium.
* Location in respect to main gate: East of the Main Gate, right along Bliss Street.

## Diana Tamari Sabbagh (DTS) Building:
* Departments/ majors: Faculty of Medicine (Basic Sciences).
* Key features: Heavy biological, anatomical, and medical research labs.
* Location in respect to main gate: Far east side of campus, serving as the bridge to the AUB Medical Center.

## Halim and Aida Daniel Academic and Clinical Center (ACC):
* Departments/ majors: Medicine and Clinics.
* Key features: Massive, modern high-rise equipped with state-of-the-art medical technology and simulation centers.
* Location in respect to main gate: Off-campus to the east, across Maamari Street.

## Rafic Hariri School of Nursing:
* Departments/ majors: Nursing.
* Key features: Specialized clinical labs, directly integrated into the hospital ecosystem.
* Location in respect to main gate: East campus, attached to the AUBMC complex.

## Saab Medical Library:
* Departments/ majors: N/A (Medical and Health Sciences Library).
* Key features: Specialized medical archives, heavily used by med and nursing students. 
* Location in respect to main gate: East campus, right by the medical/nursing buildings.

## Agriculture Building (FAFS):
* Departments/ majors: Agriculture, Food Sciences, Nutrition.
* Key features: Houses the pilot plant, specialized food testing labs, and has a dedicated FAFS food courtyard.
* Location in respect to main gate: Far west side of the upper campus.

## Biology Building & Scientific Research Building (SRB):
* Departments/ majors: FAS (Biology, core sciences).
* Key features: Contains greenhouses, natural history collections, and advanced bio-research labs.
* Location in respect to main gate: West campus, nestled near the Agriculture complex.

## Elmer & Mamdouha Bobst Chemistry Building:
* Departments/ majors: FAS (Chemistry).
* Key features: Packed with fume hoods, heavy ventilation, and wet labs.
* Location in respect to main gate: West campus, near the Biology and Physics buildings.

## Emile Bustani Physics Building (Reynolds Hall):
* Departments/ majors: FAS (Physics).
* Key features: Historic vibe with high ceilings and classical physics laboratories.
* Location in respect to main gate: West campus, close to the Green Oval.

## Lee Observatory:
* Departments/ majors: Physics/Astronomy (Historical).
* Key features: A small domed structure. It is the oldest astronomical observatory in the Middle East.
* Location in respect to main gate: Middle of the upper campus, near the Green Oval.

## Marquand House:
* Departments/ majors: N/A (President's Residence).
* Key features: Heavily guarded, surrounded by some of the most stunning and well-kept botanical gardens on campus.
* Location in respect to main gate: East of Jafet Library, tucked away on the upper campus.

## Residential Halls (Kerr, Penrose, Murex, Jewett, Boustany):
* Departments/ majors: N/A (Student Housing).
* Key features: Mix of historic and newly renovated dormitories (split by men's and women's). Strict visitation rules but offer the best views of the Mediterranean.
* Location in respect to main gate: Scattered primarily along the western and uppermost edges of campus along Bliss Street.
